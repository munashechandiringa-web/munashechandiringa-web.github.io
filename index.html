Honours Dissertation

An Integrated Geospatial Framework for Assessing Vegetation Dynamics in Mountainous Areas

Case study: Nyanga National Park, Zimbabwe (2003–2023) · Supervised by Dr. I. Gwitira, Department of Geography, Geospatial Science and Earth Observation, University of Zimbabwe.

Three Objectives

  1. 1

    Map spatiotemporal vegetation trajectories across the park, 2003–2023.

  2. 2

    Attribute the drivers of that change using Random Forest regression.

  3. 3

    Project future vegetation vulnerability under four CMIP6 SSP pathways.

Methods

  • Landsat 5/7/8/9 harmonisation & medoid compositing
  • kNDVI, EVI, NDWI index derivation
  • OLS & Mann-Kendall / Sen's slope trend analysis
  • Getis-Ord Gi* hotspot detection
  • Pearson inter-predictor screening (|r| ≥ 0.70)
  • Random Forest driver attribution, 14 predictors
  • CMIP6 SSP1-2.6 → SSP5-8.5, elevation-dependent warming
  • Monte Carlo uncertainty quantification

Validation

Classification accuracy verified with confusion matrices and Kappa coefficients; SSP projections exported as delta rasters and cross-checked with a ±0.05 residual threshold across three confidence classes.
